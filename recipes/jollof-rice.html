Jollof-Rice Recipe

Home

Description

Jollof rice is made by cooking long-grain rice in a deeply seasoned tomato and pepper sauce until the grains absorb all the bold flavors. The dish typically starts with a blended base of ripe tomatoes red bell peppers, scotch bonnet (for heat), onions, and garlic. This mixture is fried in oil to intensify the flavor and reduce excess moisture.

Tomato paste is often added to deepen the color and richness. A blend of spices—such as thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, and white pepper —along with stock (chicken, beef, or vegetable) gives the rice its signature savory taste.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5–6 medium fresh tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1–2 Scotch bonnet peppers (to taste)
  • 1 large onion (half blended, half sliced)
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger (optional)

Steps

  1. Blend the Base
  2. Fry the Sauce
  3. Season
  4. Add the Rice
  5. Steam and Finish
